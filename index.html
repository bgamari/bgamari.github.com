About

My name is Ben Gamari. I am a graduate student in the Physics Department at the University of Massachussetts Amherst where I am studying the properties of biological polymers through single-molecule spectroscopy in the group of Professor Lori Goldner. In my research I seek to further understanding of the conformational properties of biopolymers through fluorescence spectroscopy coupled with machine-learning-inspired analysis techniques.

I am also an open-source advocate, programmer, and turtle owner. In my spare time I enjoy building things, learning programming languages, listening to music, and being outdoors. I also participate in a variety of open source projects.
